Ice hockey is a competitive team sport designed to play on ice, where two teams od skaters use their sticks to compete shooting a rubber puck into their opponent’s net to score. This sport is particularly popular in Canada, the United States, along with other European countries so excitement and contention involved. A typical ice hockey teams usually consists of four lines of three forwards, three pairs of defensemen, and two goaltenders. During an actual game, each team has five players on ground and stand against the opposing team. A goaltender is usually considered as the sixth on-ice player for each team, who is responsible for keeping the puck from entering the goal.
